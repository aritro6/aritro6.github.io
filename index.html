My story

Shaped by failure.
Built by choice.

Growing up as a child of immigrants in Phoenix, I spent most of my early years trying to fit a mold that wasn't mine. School was everything — not because I loved it, but because it was the only framework I knew. I got kicked out of college. Twice.

"Why are you in school?" My advisor asked me that during what I thought was the worst meeting of my life. I had no answer. It was the first time I'd actually thought about it.

That question changed everything. I stopped performing for other people and started paying attention to what actually pulled me in. I stumbled into the startup world, worked on products for free, and slowly figured out what I was built for.

I eventually graduated — not to check a box, but because I had something to go back for. Since then I've worked as a data analyst, co-founded a software company, consulted at startups, gone deep into enterprise, and built my own practice. Every failure sharpened something. Every restart taught me what the previous chapter couldn't.

And through all of it — since 4th grade — I've been a drummer. Two very different worlds. The same instinct underneath.

The through-line

Every good sprint retrospective and every good jazz solo has the same thing in common: honest listening, then a clear response. I've never been able to fully separate the two worlds — and I've stopped trying.
