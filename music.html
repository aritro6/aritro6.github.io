The story

Two decades
behind the kit.

I started playing drums in 4th grade. I don't remember choosing it — it just made sense immediately. Middle school gave me concert band and jazz band. High school gave me everything: concert band, jazz band, winter drumline, marching band, percussion ensemble, and steel band. Music was the place where I always felt most like myself.

College slowed things down. Life got complicated and I stepped away from the kit for about four years. When I came back, I came back differently — with my own money, my own time, and a much clearer sense of why it mattered to me. I took lessons again, reinvested seriously in my gear, and started showing up to jam sessions at The Nash in downtown Phoenix.

Taking that four-year break taught me more about why I play than any lesson could have.

The Nash sessions were a turning point. Jazz demands real listening — you can't fake it in a room with other musicians. Every cymbal choice, every brush stroke, every moment of restraint is a conversation. That's where I figured out what kind of player I wanted to be.

Now I teach. The same things that made me a better player — listening first, responding with intention, not overcomplicating it — are the same things I try to pass on to students.

Teaching

How I teach
and what we work on.

My approach to teaching is rooted in jazz, which means it starts with listening. Before technique, before speed, before anything — I want students to hear what the music is asking for. That's the foundation everything else is built on.

I work with beginners who have never touched a drum, intermediate players who've hit a wall, and anyone in between. I don't have a one-size-fits-all curriculum. Every student gets something built around where they are and where they want to go.

I also teach intro piano — enough to understand music theory, find your way around a keyboard, and play something that sounds like music. Same philosophy: feel it first, then formalize it.

Lessons available at Guitar Center (ask at the drum counter) and privately through my music school site.
