How I got here

The career arc,
honestly told.

I didn't plan to become a Scrum Master. I co-founded Rayo at ASU's Luminosity Lab — a software platform to help people with developmental disabilities communicate more effectively — and suddenly I was running a team of developers with no playbook. I picked up Scrum out of necessity and it clicked immediately.

Before that I was a data analyst at The Million Dollar Teacher Project, helping teachers make sense of data that was supposed to help them do their jobs better. Before that I was in and out of college, working at startups for free, figuring out what I was actually built for.

After Rayo, I went into consulting — working with startups like ElevateU and Permascape, helping them build processes they could actually sustain. Then enterprise software, where the problems were bigger and the stakes were different. Then I built my own practice: Unblock Agency.

The through-line across all of it: I'm good at seeing where teams get stuck and helping them move. Whether that's a sprint planning problem, a release coordination mess, or a team that's lost trust in its own process — I've usually seen a version of it before.
